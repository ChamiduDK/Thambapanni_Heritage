Sri Lankan Painting Heritage

The artistic legacy of Sri Lanka spans over two millennia, showcasing remarkable evolution in techniques, styles, and cultural influences. From the ancient Sigiriya frescoes to the intricate temple murals, each era contributed uniquely to our rich artistic heritage.

The Anuradhapura period marked the beginning of Buddhist influence in art, while the Polonnaruwa era brought new dimensions to religious paintings. The Kandyan period later introduced distinctive local styles that continue to influence modern artists.

These masterpieces demonstrate advanced understanding of natural pigments, perspective, and storytelling through visual art, making Sri Lankan painting tradition one of the most sophisticated in South Asia.

Traditional Painting Techniques

Tempera Painting

Ancient egg-based paint technique used in temple murals, creating vibrant and long-lasting colors.

  • Natural pigments
  • Egg-based binder
  • Durable finish

Fresco Painting

Wall painting technique on wet lime plaster, seen in Sigiriya and ancient temple artwork.

  • Wet plaster base
  • Multiple layers
  • Weather resistant

Mineral Pigments

Traditional color extraction from natural minerals, creating authentic Sri Lankan art colors.

  • Natural minerals
  • Hand-ground
  • Rich colors
